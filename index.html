Post your own video clips to your own TikTok — after a preview and a click.

TT Automation is a personal desktop application. It uploads finished video clips that you select to your own TikTok account, and only after you preview the clip and confirm. Nothing is posted automatically or without your explicit confirmation.

What it does

Preview before posting

Every clip is shown with a preview, the connected account, a caption, and a privacy selector before anything is sent.

You stay in control

Comments, duet and stitch are off by default; you choose the privacy level. The post only happens when you click confirm.

Your own accounts

It only ever acts on TikTok accounts that you personally authorize via TikTok's official login.

How it works

  1. Connect your account. You authorize your own TikTok account through TikTok's official Login Kit (OAuth). You can revoke access anytime in your TikTok settings.
  2. Pick a clip and review. A review screen shows a preview of the clip, your account name, the caption, the privacy level, and interaction settings.
  3. Confirm to post. When you choose a privacy level and click confirm, the app uploads and posts the clip you confirmed — and only that clip.

How it uses TikTok

Login Kit

Used so the account owner can securely authorize their own account (scope user.info.basic).

Content Posting API

Used to upload the selected video and, after your confirmation, publish it to your account (video.publish / video.upload).

The app does not collect or share any data with third parties and does not run analytics. Access tokens are stored locally on the owner's own computer. See the Privacy Policy for details.

FAQ

Who is this for?
It is single-user software, run locally by its owner to post to their own TikTok account(s).
Does it post anything automatically?
No. Every post requires an on-screen preview and an explicit confirmation by the owner.
How do I revoke access?
In your TikTok app under Settings → Security & permissions → Manage app permissions, or by deleting the app's local credential file.
